A Space Odyssey

Mars, the red planet, named for the Roman God of War, is the fourth largest from the sun, consisting of large rock and a nickel-backed surface. For thousands of years, scientists have long been plagued with the mystery surrounding Mars's surface and the potential for Mars to harbor human life. In the 21st Century, scientists from Planet Earth constructed a space voyager named Rover to inspect the particles and material of the red planet. What the Rover eventually found were etchings resembling veins in what were once streams that ran on the planet, posing several more questions about what the now frigid planet could have been before its evolution. Because there are no magnetic fields on Mars, the radiation has made the planet uninhabitable, particularly during those dry dead Martian winters, notes Space.com.
